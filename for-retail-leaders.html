You hired a lighting designer. Why not a sound designer?

Your team spent months on lighting, fixtures, and merchandising. Your soundtrack is a two-year-old Spotify playlist nobody remembers choosing.

Your Store Has a Blind Spot

You've walked into your own store and heard a song that didn't belong. Lyrics that clashed with the brand. Energy that didn't match the customer in front of you. You noticed. Your customers noticed too.

Visual Design is Intentional

Every piece of visual merchandising reflects your brand. Your lighting is calculated for ambiance. Your color palette sends a message. Your store is a carefully composed environment.

Audio is an Afterthought

Your soundtrack is a Spotify playlist someone picked two years ago. Or worse, generic content from Mood Media or Soundtrack Your Brand, organized by mood-tags that have nothing to do with your customer's psychology. And those lyrics? Nobody screened them. Your brand has no control over what words play in your stores.

"Upbeat" is not a customer profile. "Indie rock" is not a strategy.

Generic playlists carry real risk. Unscreened lyrics can conflict with your brand values. A playlist built for "chill vibes" doesn't distinguish between your 22-year-old browsing clearance and your 45-year-old buying a sofa. Your employees hear it 2,000 hours a year, 10x more than any customer. A 3-4 hour loop means they hear the same songs 50 times per month.

What Changes With Entuned

We Profile Your Ideal Customer

Not demographics. Psychology. What do they value? What's their aesthetic? What music shaped them? What makes them feel premium? We build a psychographic model that becomes your soundtrack's DNA.

We Create Original Music

Built specifically for that person. Not pulled from a catalog. Every track is original composition created for your customer's psychology, values, and sensibilities. Every lyric is intentional. Every word aligns with your brand.

Your Audio Becomes as Intentional as Your Visuals

The same rigor you apply to merchandising, lighting, and design now applies to your acoustic environment. Every element aligned. One cohesive experience.

Multi-Zone: Different Departments, Different Soundtracks

West Elm has a $40 dorm pillow section and a $3,000 sofa section. Those customers need different music. The 19-year-old shopping for dorm decor responds to pop-rock energy at 110 BPM. The 38-year-old buying a luxury sofa responds to ambient sophistication at 65 BPM. Only original, created-to-order music makes simultaneous multi-zone soundtracks possible. Catalog-based providers are architecturally locked into one playlist per store.

Research gives your CFO the numbers. Areni and Kim found that congruent music increases willingness to pay by 8-12%.
