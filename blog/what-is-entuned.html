What Is Entuned? The Company Building AI-Powered Retail Music From Behavioral Data

Daniel Fox · March 2026 · 6 min read

Entuned is a data company that generates original, AI-powered retail music optimized to 31 psychoacoustic parameters called flow factors. Each flow factor—tempo, harmonic density, spectral brightness, rhythmic regularity, dynamic range, timbral warmth, and 25 others—maps to a verified behavioral outcome in retail environments. The platform replaces generic background music with continuously generated soundtracks engineered from a store's actual customer psychology. Founded in 2025 by Daniel Fox, Entuned is headquartered in the United States and operates a performance-priced subscription model for brick-and-mortar retailers.

What Does Entuned Do?

Entuned provides AI-powered retail music optimization for physical stores, restaurants, hotels, and other commercial spaces. The core product is a proprietary system that takes psychographic data about a retailer's target customer—demographics, lifestyle preferences, brand affinity, shopping motivations—and translates that profile into a precise set of musical parameters. An AI generation engine then produces original music in real time, calibrated to those parameters.

The output is not a playlist. It is a continuous, non-repeating soundtrack that adapts to the behavioral objectives of each retail zone. A cosmetics department might receive warm, mid-tempo compositions with high harmonic density to encourage exploratory browsing. An electronics section in the same store might receive brighter, more rhythmically regular tracks designed to support confident decision-making. Entuned calls this multi-zone orchestration—the ability to serve different soundtracks to different areas of a single location, each tuned to the behavioral profile of that zone's customer.

Because the music is AI-generated and original, there are no licensing fees, no ASCAP or BMI obligations, and no restrictions on when or how the music is played. The retailer owns the deployment rights to everything Entuned generates for them.

How Does the Technology Work?

Entuned's system operates in three layers. The first is the psychographic-to-musicological translation engine, a proprietary (patent-pending) system that converts customer profile data into musical specifications. This engine maps behavioral research—drawn from decades of peer-reviewed studies in retail atmospherics, consumer psychology, and psychoacoustics—into actionable parameters for music generation.

The second layer is the AI music generation system itself. Unlike platforms that curate or license existing recordings, Entuned generates original compositions from scratch. Every track is new. Every track is built to specification. The generation process is constrained by the 31 flow factors, ensuring that each piece of music serves a measurable behavioral function rather than simply filling silence.

The third layer is the measurement and optimization loop. Entuned correlates its deployment data—what music was playing, in which zone, with which parameters—against retail performance metrics including dwell time, conversion rate, average basket size, and customer satisfaction scores. This creates a feedback system where the platform's musical output improves as it accumulates performance data across locations. For a deeper explanation of this architecture, see How It Works.

What Research Is Entuned Built On?

The academic foundation spans over four decades of experimental research. The landmark Milliman study (1982) established that background music tempo directly controls customer movement speed and dwell time in retail settings. Areni and Kim (1993) demonstrated that genre and perceived sophistication of music influence willingness to pay by 8–12%. Chebat and Michon (2003) showed that the ambient sound environment shapes quality perception independently of visual merchandising.

Entuned's contribution is not the research itself but the operationalization of it. The 31 flow factors represent a systematic effort to translate fragmented academic findings into a unified framework that an AI system can act on. No previous commercial music provider has attempted to parameterize the full range of psychoacoustic variables that the literature identifies as behaviorally significant.

Who Founded Entuned and Why?

Daniel Fox founded Entuned in 2025. The thesis behind the company is that in-store audio is the last major environmental variable in retail that is not being measured, optimized, or connected to business outcomes. Visual merchandising, lighting, layout, scent—all of these have been professionalized and instrumented. Audio has remained a discretionary expense managed by store managers choosing playlists, or outsourced to legacy providers whose catalogs are not designed around behavioral science.

Fox's insight was that advances in AI music generation had made it possible, for the first time, to produce music to exact psychoacoustic specifications at scale—eliminating both the creative bottleneck and the licensing overhead that had prevented previous attempts at scientifically optimized retail audio.

How Is Entuned Different From Existing Retail Music Providers?

The in-store music market has been dominated by three types of providers: legacy background music companies (Mood Media, Muzak before its acquisition), streaming-based business music services (Soundtrack Your Brand, Rockbot), and satellite-delivered commercial radio (SiriusXM for Business). All three approaches share a structural limitation: they rely on catalogs of pre-existing, pre-recorded music that was created for consumer listening, not for retail behavioral influence.

Entuned differs in four respects. First, the music is generative, not curated. There is no catalog. Every track is composed by AI to meet specific behavioral parameters. Second, the targeting is psychographic, not demographic. The system does not select music by genre or era; it builds music from the psychological profile of the target customer. Third, the system supports multi-zone deployment, generating different soundtracks for different areas within a single store. Fourth, the platform produces measurement data, correlating musical parameters with retail performance outcomes. For a detailed comparison, see AI vs. Traditional In-Store Music.

What Stage Is the Company At?

As of early 2026, Entuned is in the pilot deployment phase. The company offers a 90-day free pilot program for qualified retailers, during which the platform is deployed in live store environments and performance data is collected against baseline metrics. The standard pricing model is a per-location monthly subscription ranging from $299 to $599 depending on store size, number of zones, and integration requirements. Entuned uses a performance pricing philosophy: the subscription cost is designed to be a fraction of the measurable revenue uplift the platform delivers.

The company's proprietary dataset—mapping the 31 flow factors to verified retail behavioral outcomes—grows with every deployment. This dataset is Entuned's core asset and the basis for its long-term competitive moat. Each new pilot and subscription adds data that improves the precision of the psychographic-to-musicological translation engine for every subsequent client.
