The Science Behind Our Projections

Milliman (1982)

Established the foundational link between tempo, tempo variability, and dwell time in retail environments.

Smith & Curnow (1966)

Demonstrated that music perception is closely tied to customer mood and purchasing behavior.

Areni & Kim (1993)

Showed that music-induced mood directly correlates with increased spending and willingness to pay premium prices.

North et al. (1999)

Found that congruence between music and customer identity primes willingness to spend more and increases brand perception.

Chebat & Michon (2003)

Established that refined audio environments signal premium positioning and increase the perceived value of products and services.
