Retail Audio Has Never Been Measured. Until Now.

Background music providers sell playlists. Entuned measures what happens when the music changes.

The Existing Options

Retailers currently choose from three categories of audio solution. Licensed playlist services aggregate commercial music into themed collections and handle the licensing paperwork. Mood-based curation tools let managers pick from preset categories organized by energy level, genre, or time of day. Background music providers deliver a managed stream designed to fill silence without attracting attention.

Each of these categories optimizes for a different version of the same goal: making sure the music is not objectionable. The selection criteria are listener preference, brand alignment by genre, and licensing compliance. The question they answer is "what should we play." None of them ask what happened after they played it.

These tools treat audio as a fixed input. Pick a playlist, press play, move on. There is no feedback loop. There is no measurement. There is no mechanism to determine whether the audio environment helped or hurt the business on any given day.

What's Missing

Existing solutions treat music as either a cost center or a brand signal. A line item for compliance, or a creative expression of store identity. In both cases, the music is chosen and the job is considered done.

What none of them do is close the loop between what plays and what customers do. No existing retail audio provider measures dwell time changes, transaction value shifts, or traffic pattern differences as a function of the audio environment. The connection between soundtrack and commercial outcome is assumed, never verified.

This means every retail audio decision is made without evidence. Millions of dollars spent annually on solutions that have never demonstrated measurable impact on the metrics retailers actually care about.

How Entuned Is Different

Entuned measures behavioral outcomes, not listener preference. The question is not whether customers like the music. The question is whether they stayed longer, spent more, and came back.

Every deployment generates data that improves the next optimization. Each store's performance feeds a growing dataset connecting musical parameters to commercial outcomes across retail environments.

The system gets more accurate over time. As the dataset grows, the relationship between specific musical variables and specific behavioral outcomes becomes more precise. Every retailer benefits from the aggregate learning.
