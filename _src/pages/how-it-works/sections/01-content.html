Tempo & Dwell Time

Slower tempos (60–80 BPM) increase shopping time and deeper browsing. Faster tempos accelerate decisions and reduce time-in-store. We compose tempo specifically for your customer's psychology.

Smith & Curnow, 1966 — Journal of Applied Psychology

8–12%
higher willingness to pay

Genre Congruence

When music matches a customer's identity, they feel "seen" by the space. Willingness to pay increases measurably. When it mismatches, they leave faster — no matter how good the products are.

Areni & Kim, 1993; North, Hargreaves & McKendrick, 1999

↑
perceived value

Luxury Priming

Refined instrumental music primes luxury perception, even in non-luxury segments. You don't need to be luxury to benefit. The right music raises your customer's perception of what your products are worth.

Chebat & Michon, 2003 — Journal of Business Research

↓
browsing time at high volume

Volume & Arousal

Lower volume encourages browsing and longer visits. Higher volume creates urgency and faster turnover. The right level depends on your purchase cycle — considered purchases need space to think.

Caldwell & Hibbert, 2002 — Psychology & Marketing

✓
intentional audio = intentional brand

The Brand Halo Effect

When music feels right for a space, customers assume everything else is right too — the products, the brand, the quality. Music acts as a signal of intentionality. Generic playlists signal carelessness.
