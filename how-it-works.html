From psychology to soundtrack in four steps.

We don't pick songs from a catalog. We build soundtracks from your customer's psychology — created fresh, measured in real stores.

Step 01

We profile your ideal customer.

Not demographics. Psychology. What do they value? What's their aesthetic? What makes them feel premium? Through a detailed onboarding conversation, we build a psychographic model of your ICP. This becomes the DNA of your store's soundtrack.

Step 02

Our engine translates psychology into music.

Your ICP becomes a set of musical instructions. Our proprietary translation layer converts psychological attributes into musicological parameters — tempo, key, instrumentation, harmonic complexity, genre DNA. Every psychographic dimension maps to a musical choice.

Step 03

Original music is created for your store.

Every track is an original composition, built for your customer's psychology. No licensing fees. No ASCAP compliance. No catalog constraints. Your music is as unique as your customer — and it evolves as we learn what works.

Step 04

Stream it. Measure it. Learn what works.

Connect to your speakers and press play. Your store's soundtrack streams continuously — original music, generated for your customer, running 24/7. We measure everything. Dwell time, foot traffic, sales data. Every hour of music in your store teaches us what's working and what to adjust. The goal: a clear, data-backed picture of how your audio environment affects your business.

The Research

The science behind the engine.

Five behavioral variables. Decades of peer-reviewed research. Each one maps to a musical decision.

15–25%
longer visits

Tempo & Dwell Time

Slower tempos (60–80 BPM) increase shopping time and deeper browsing. Faster tempos accelerate decisions and reduce time-in-store. We compose tempo specifically for your customer's psychology.

Smith & Curnow, 1966 — Journal of Applied Psychology

8–12%
higher willingness to pay

Genre Congruence

When music matches a customer's identity, they feel "seen" by the space. Willingness to pay increases measurably. When it mismatches, they leave faster — no matter how good the products are.

Areni & Kim, 1993; North, Hargreaves & McKendrick, 1999

↑
perceived value

Luxury Priming

Refined instrumental music primes luxury perception, even in non-luxury segments. You don't need to be luxury to benefit. The right music raises your customer's perception of what your products are worth.

Chebat & Michon, 2003 — Journal of Business Research

↓
browsing time at high volume

Volume & Arousal

Lower volume encourages browsing and longer visits. Higher volume creates urgency and faster turnover. The right level depends on your purchase cycle — considered purchases need space to think.

Caldwell & Hibbert, 2002 — Psychology & Marketing

✓
intentional audio = intentional brand

The Brand Halo Effect

When music feels right for a space, customers assume everything else is right too — the products, the brand, the quality. Music acts as a signal of intentionality. Generic playlists signal carelessness.
